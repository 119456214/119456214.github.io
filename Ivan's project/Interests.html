Interests

Sports

Although I am no longer able to play sports I still have a big interest in them. Up until I
was 16 years old I was a heavily involved in Gaelic Football and Camogie. I played Gaelic with both my
school and my local club. Most of the time I played full-forward but now and again i was put in midfield.
For camogie I played as a centre-back. Unfortunately during a Gaelic match I tore my Acl which put an end
to both sports. Now I am a member of NRG Fitness Gym in Little Island and train there regularly. The gym
is a huge part of my life now, during the Leaving Cert it gave me a well needed break from studying
and stressing over exams, I found it the best way to get a break from the books. Now I go to the
gym after college up to 5 times a week. I find sports and exercise very important, not only for health
reasons but also for mental reasons. Exercise is the best way to relieve stress and relax in my opinion.

Travel

Travelling has been a big interest of mine ever since I was young. Travelling has given me the opportunity to
see and experience a variety of different cultures. When I was two years old my parents took me to Alicante in Spain
and I have been going there ever since. My family owns a villa in Alicante which gives me the opportunity to go there
when I please. This has also given me the chance to learn Spanish and I now consider myself to have a very good
understanding of the language. Another place I had the opportunity to visit was Liverpool. In January, myself and a
friend took a trip there. We got the chance to explore Anfield Stadium, see the Radio City Tower and of course
experience the night-life. In the near future I hope to visit America, either on a J1 or on placement. In my opinion I think
everyone who has the opportuinty to travel should act on it, especially at my age. Young people should travel and explore the
world before settling down and working full-time. I myself am hoping to go travelling this summer with my friends.
